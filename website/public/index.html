01 — The premise

A laboratory for the origin of form & behaviour.

AEONS is a research project built to simulate the evolution of biological form and behaviour, grounded in the two great theories of evolution — those of Charles Darwin and Jean-Baptiste de Lamarck.

It focuses on multicellularity — the emergence of shape from many simple cells — and on how organisms behave toward their environment as the generations pass. The ambition is a complex world with realistic parameters, where scientifically proven concepts about evolution can be studied as they unfold, live.

02 — Two engines of change

Darwin & Lamarck, as a switch you can flip.

Two evolution modes drive everything you observe. Toggle between them to feel the difference.

Natural selection, and nothing else.

In Darwin Mode, blind variation and differential survival are the only driving factors for everything that can be observed. Mutations arrive at random; the world decides who reproduces. No organism knows what it is becoming.

  • Random mutation of the genome
  • Survival of the fittest body plans
  • Behaviour shaped purely by who lives to reproduce

Neural networks that steer their own genes.

In Lamarck Mode, small neural networks decide how to slowly edit an organism's genetic information so that survival is best secured. Acquired advantage feeds back into heredity — evolution with a sense of direction.

  • Per-organism brains propose genetic edits
  • Adaptation accumulates within and across lifetimes
  • Goal-seeking change, not blind chance
03 — The life forms

Bodies built from cells. Minds built from math.

Every life form is assembled from cells — simple geometric shapes — and piloted by a small, isolated machine-learning brain.

Geometric cells

Two kinds of cell — light-harvesting Photo and structural Non-Photo — snap together on a crystalline lattice to compose every body part.

Emergent multicellularity

From single seed cells, organisms grow body parts, branch into limbs, and settle into symmetries. Shape is never hand-drawn — it is grown.

Isolated brains

Behaviour rests on small, independent ML algorithms — one brain per organism — that learn to chase food, flee, and reproduce. Their learning, too, is dictated by the active evolution mode.

Heritable genomes

A compact genome encodes body plan and behaviour. Offspring inherit it — mutated by chance in Darwin Mode, edited with intent in Lamarck Mode.

04 — The world

A terrain authored by hand, ruled by physics.

To guarantee maximum control over the environment, the terrain is constructed from two hand-crafted images:

Heightmap

defines the shape of the land — valleys, ridges, the climb of every slope.

Material map

The aim is a complex world with realistic parameters, in which proven mechanisms of evolution can be studied in real time.

05 — Built to run for aeons

Engineered for the long run.

To run the simulation for a very long time without crashing, AEONS is built on a deliberately robust stack.

language

Rust

Memory-safe and fast — the foundation that keeps the simulation stable across endless generations.

engine

Bevy

A modern, data-driven game engine providing efficient rendering and an ECS world for thousands of organisms.

learning

Burn

Drives the organisms' neural networks directly on the GPU, so brains learn while the world keeps turning.

Where it goes next

If the project succeeds, a larger version will extend the simulation to the evolution of biochemical aspects — chemistry as a new dimension of natural selection.

Evolution, accelerated.

AEONS is an open research instrument for the patient study of life's oldest process — running on your own machine, for as long as you let it.
